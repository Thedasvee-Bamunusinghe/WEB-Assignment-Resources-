Sustainable Development Goals

The UN Department of Economics and Social Affairs with the United Nations worked for decades since June 1992 with the Agenda 21 adopting more than 178 countries up until September 2015 where the post 2015 development agenda was negotiated at the United Nations General Assembly and adopted on setting up achieving a collection of 17 goals by the year 2030 with the intention of being a "blueprint to achieve a better and a more sustainable future for all".
These goals were named as the 17 Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs).


THE 17 SDG GOALS

  1. End poverty in all its forms everywhere.
  2. End hunger, achieve food security and improved nutrition and promote sustainable agriculture.
  3. Ensure healthy lives and promote well-being for all at all ages.
  4. Ensure inclusive and equitable quality education and promote lifelong learning opportunities for all.
  5. Achieve gender equality and empower all women and girls.
  6. Ensure availability and sustainable management of water and sanitation for all.
  7. Ensure access to affordable, reliable, sustainable, and modern energy for all.
  8. Promote sustained, inclusive, and sustainable economic growth, full and productive employment and decent work for all.
  9. Build resilient infastructure, promote inclusive and sustainable industralization and faster innovation.
  10. Reduce inequality within and among countries.
  11. Make cities and human settlements inclusive, safe, resilient, and sustainable.
  12. Ensure sustainable consumption and production patterns.
  13. Take urgent action to combat climate change and its impacts.
  14. Conserve and sustainably use the oceans, seas, and marine resources for sustainable development.
  15. Protect, restore and promote sustainable use of terrestrial ecosystems, sustainably manage forests, combat desertification, and hault and reserve land degradation and halt biodiversity loss.
  16. Strengthen the means of implementation and revitalize the global partnership for sustainable development.

As of the 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development the main scope is in consideration of People, Planet, Prosperity, Peace and Partnership. The main aim of achieving these 17 goals was pointed out in the 2030 Agenda as to benefit the scope parties mentioned.
The Sustainabale Development Goals was signed by 193 countries globally, where they do track the progress of each country within their 3 dimensions of Environment, Social and Economic Developments. In long term, the SGDs will be a major factor to identify challenges, success turn and gaps within the countries supporting the partnerships on identifying the best solutions and best practises. The agenda can also be known as the people centered process which would give the authority for human rights and gender sensitivity particularly focusing on the broken, vulnerable and the low rated citizens out there.
